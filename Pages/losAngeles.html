Best Things to do in Los Angeles, California

PLACES TO SEE

  • Venice Boardwalk: Venice boardwalk is one of the liveliest spots at Venice, Los Angeles. You will see street vendors and performers through this spot. You will be able to see a true mix of what makes up Los Angeles by spending a day here.
  • Grauman's Chinese Theatre: Feel the feet and hand prints of hundreds of stars that have come and gone. Preserved forever in front of the theatre, this spot attracts millions of tourists every year. The tourists are from around the world. It is a good place where you can also take your family and enjoy the day there.
  • Hollywood Walk of Fame: Take a walk down the star studded path to see the official star plaques awarded to celebrities over the years. This is a place to visit if you happen to go to Los Angeles, you have a great time here.
  • Griffith Park: Griffith park stretches over 4000 acres of land and is one of the largest municipal parks in the country. You should visit this place for a nice picnic or a quiet stroll to gather your thoughts.

THINGS TO DO

  • Shopping: Grand Central Market, Central Plaza Chinatown and Malibu Country Mart are perfect brewing spots to finding the best deals on the best items.
  • Eateries: Simply walk the streets to find caf√©s and restaurants that offer up the best in American fries and burgers or even pizzas. Try Camponile or Europane for a more comprehensive menu.
  • Nightlife: The Mandrake, Polo Lounge and Echo are a few of the popular hot spots in the city for celebrities, star watchers and tourists alike. There are also casinos everywhere and if you are a gambler, then Los Angeles is one place you should visit.

California Science Center

California Science Center Travel Tips

  • Visit early on weekends to avoid crowds.
  • Prior reservation is recommended.
  • Group tours are available.
  • Admission to the permanent exhibitions of California Science Center is free.
  • Parking is available at the premises.

How to Reach California Science Center

  • Take the Metro Expo Line to the USC Station which is just a 5-minute walk away from the museum.
  • If you are traveling from outside LA County, then take the Amtrak or Metrolink to Union Station in downtown Los Angeles.
